# Vecinita English FAQs

## What is Vecinita?

Vecinita is a community-based Q&A assistant designed to help people find information about local services, community programs, and resources in Rhode Island, with a focus on the Woonasquatucket River Watershed area.

## How does this work?

Vecinita works by searching through a database of community documents and resources to provide accurate answers. It uses advanced AI technology to understand your questions and find relevant information from verified sources.

## Who created Vecinita?

Vecinita is an open-source project created to support community information access and environmental education. It was developed in collaboration with the Woonasquatucket River Watershed Council.

## What is the Woonasquatucket River Watershed Council?

The Woonasquatucket River Watershed Council (WRWC) is a non-profit organization dedicated to protecting and restoring the Woonasquatucket River and its watershed through community engagement, education, and environmental stewardship.

## What is water quality?

Water quality refers to the chemical, physical, and biological characteristics of water. It measures how suitable water is for various uses such as drinking, recreation, and supporting aquatic life. Key indicators include pH, dissolved oxygen, temperature, turbidity, and the presence of pollutants or contaminants.

## Why is watershed protection important?

Watershed protection is important because watersheds supply our drinking water, support wildlife habitats, provide recreational opportunities, and protect communities from flooding. Healthy watersheds filter pollutants, reduce erosion, and maintain the natural water cycle that all life depends on.

## What areas does WRWC serve?

WRWC serves the Woonasquatucket River Watershed, which covers approximately 51 square miles in Rhode Island, including portions of Providence, North Providence, Johnston, Cranston, and Smithfield.

## Are there educational programs for schools?

Yes, WRWC offers educational programs for schools including watershed science lessons, field trips to the river, water quality testing demonstrations, and environmental education workshops tailored to different grade levels.

## What is stormwater runoff?

Stormwater runoff is water from rain or melting snow that flows over land surfaces instead of soaking into the ground. As it flows, it picks up pollutants like oil, chemicals, trash, and sediment, carrying them into storm drains and eventually into rivers and streams.

## How can I help reduce water pollution?

You can help reduce water pollution by:
- Properly disposing of chemicals, oil, and pet waste
- Reducing lawn fertilizer and pesticide use
- Installing rain gardens or rain barrels
- Keeping storm drains clear of debris
- Participating in local cleanup events
- Supporting green infrastructure projects

## What wildlife lives in the watershed?

The Woonasquatucket River Watershed supports diverse wildlife including fish species (alewife, eel, trout), birds (herons, kingfishers, ducks), mammals (beaver, otter, muskrat), and various insects, amphibians, and plants.

## Is the river safe for recreation?

Water quality in the Woonasquatucket River varies by location and weather conditions. WRWC conducts regular water quality monitoring. Check current conditions and advisories before swimming or wading. The river is generally safe for non-contact recreation like kayaking and fishing.

## What is green infrastructure?

Green infrastructure refers to natural or nature-based systems that manage stormwater, improve water quality, and provide environmental benefits. Examples include rain gardens, bioswales, permeable pavement, green roofs, and urban trees.

## How do I report pollution?

To report pollution or environmental concerns:
- For immediate spills or emergencies: Call RI DEM Emergency Response at (401) 222-3070
- For non-emergency concerns: Contact WRWC at info@wrwc.org
- For storm drain issues: Contact your local public works department

## What is a rain garden?

A rain garden is a planted depression that absorbs rainwater runoff from roofs, driveways, and other impervious surfaces. It helps filter pollutants, reduce flooding, and recharge groundwater while providing habitat for wildlife.

## Can I kayak on the river?

Yes, kayaking and canoeing are popular activities on the Woonasquatucket River. Several access points are available. Always check current water levels and weather conditions, wear a life jacket, and be aware of any dams or obstacles.
